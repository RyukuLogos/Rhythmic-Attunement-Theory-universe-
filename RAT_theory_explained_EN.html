1. Overview & Intuition

RAT says: the tension energy required to maintain a deviation from perfect phase closure becomes mass. The minimal form is

$$\boxed{mc^2 = A\,\theta^2}$$

Here $\theta$ is the phase deviation (dimensionless angle) from closure, and $A$ is the energy scale of the tension that keeps the deviation. Intuitively: if a wave cannot close and self-overlap neatly in one turn, it must store energy proportional to that misfit.

Axiom A (Normalization)

Axiom B (Dimensionless Fingerprint)

From the tension decomposition a dimensionless product appears:
$$\boxed{\Xi \equiv M\,M_P^2 = 2}$$

With $M_P^2=\hbar c/G$. The value $\Xi=2$ emerges as a “fingerprint” that simultaneously satisfies independent requirements (Maxwell $c$, tension form, and the critical closure length).

  • Quantum constant: $\hbar$
  • Electromagnetic constants: $\varepsilon_0,\ \mu_0$ ($c^2=1/(\varepsilon_0\mu_0)$)
  • Cell-count coefficient: $\alpha$ (cells per unit mass)
Bottom line: fixing the axioms ($\kappa=1,\ \theta=\sqrt{m},\ \Xi=2$) makes $L,\ c,\ \alpha$ etc. fall out as results, not inputs.

3. Shortest Derivation

0) Cast

  • Phase deviation: $\theta$ (dimensionless)
  • Tension scale: $A$ (energy)
  • Goal (minimal form): $\boxed{mc^2 = A\,\theta^2}$

1) Schrödinger kinetic term ⇒ tension from phase gradient

$$E_{\rm cell}\approx \frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\left(\frac{\theta}{L}\right)^2$$

Using the phase gradient $\nabla\phi\!\sim\!\theta/L$ over a cell length $L$.

2) Bundle of cells = particle; match rest energy

$$mc^2=N_{\rm cell}(m)\,\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\theta^2}{L^2} \quad\Rightarrow\quad mc^2=A\,\theta^2,$$ $$A\equiv \alpha\frac{\hbar^2}{2L^2},\ \ N_{\rm cell}(m)=\alpha m$$

3) Adopt the empirical rule $\;\theta=\kappa\sqrt{m}$

$$mc^2=A\,\kappa^2 m \ \Rightarrow\ \boxed{c^2=A\,\kappa^2}$$

4) Connect to Maxwell

$$c^2=\frac{1}{\varepsilon_0\mu_0},\qquad A=\frac{\hbar^2}{2}\frac{M}{L^2} \ \Rightarrow\ M=\frac{2L^2}{\hbar^2\kappa^2\varepsilon_0\mu_0}$$

4. Fixing the Constants & the Dimensionless Fingerprint

From the axioms ($\kappa=1,\ \Xi=2$) the critical closure length is fixed to $L=\ell_P$, and

$$A=\frac{\hbar^2}{2}\frac{M}{L^2}=c^2,\qquad \Xi=M\,M_P^2=2$$

hold. Hence $A=c^2$, consistent with $c^2=1/(\varepsilon_0\mu_0)$. The cell-count coefficient $\alpha$ follows as

$$c^2=\frac{\hbar^2}{2L^2}\,\alpha\,\kappa^2 \quad(\kappa=1,\ L=\ell_P)\ \Rightarrow\ \boxed{\alpha=\frac{2\ell_P^2}{\hbar^2\varepsilon_0\mu_0}}$$

Result: $\{L,\hbar,c,\alpha,\varepsilon_0,\mu_0\}$ are jointly fixed—invariants.

6. What “Cells” Mean

A “cell” is the minimal analytical unit over which phase deviation energy can be evaluated. It is not a literal substructure but a computational block for the local phase gradient energy.

$$E_{\rm cell}\approx \frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\left(\frac{\theta}{L}\right)^2,\qquad E_{\rm rest}=N_{\rm cell}(m)\,E_{\rm cell}$$

With $N_{\rm cell}\propto m$ the minimal form $mc^2=A\theta^2$ follows.
